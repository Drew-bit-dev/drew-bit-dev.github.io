Ingredients:

  • 1/4c Cornstarch
  • 2/3c Sugar
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 2c Scalded milk
  • 3 Lightly beaten egg yolks
  • 3 Stiff beaten egg whites
  • 1c Coconut shavings
  • 2 tbs Melted butter
  • 1/2 tsp Vanilla
  • 6 tbs Sugar
  • 1/4 tsp Cream of Tartar
  • 9" Pie shell (prebaked)

Instructions:

Custard:

  1. Mix cornstarch, sugar and salt together
  2. Scald the milk and slowly add to the mixture
  3. Cook in a double boiler until thickened (~10 to 30 mins) STIRRING CONSTANTLY
  4. If it is not thickening enough over the heat, slowly add 1 tbs of cornstarchat a time until thickened, stirring constantly
  5. Slowly add a small amount of the mixture to the egg yolks, and then add that back intothe mixture
  6. Cook on double boiler again for around 5 minutes
  7. Allow to cool, add melted butter, vanilla and coconut shavings
  8. Pour into the cooled prebaked pie shell
  9. Set in fridge for 1-2 hours minimum

Meringue:

  • Beat the egg whites, sugar and cream of tartar and vanilla together
  • Beat until stiff peaks are formed when the mixer is pulled out of the mixture
  • Use a hand mixer on medium to high speed

Finishing the Pie:

  • Spread the meringue onto the set pie and sprinkle with coconut shavings
  • Bake at 350° for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown

Ingredients:

  • 2c Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2c Wesson oil
  • 1/4c Milk (1% or 2%)

Directions:

  1. Mix ingredients together in a large bowl
  2. Use hands to form two balls
  3. Roll out between two sheets of wax paper

Notes:

  • If the crust is too dry, add small amounts of water to mixture until desired consistency
  • for coconut cream pie, use both balls of dough

Ingredients:

  • 1/2c Butter
  • 2oz 100% cocoa solid bakers chocolate
  • 1c Sugar
  • 2 Eggs
  • 3/4c Flour
  • 1tsp Vanilla
  • Dash salt
  • 1/2c Nuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°
  2. Melt butter and chocolate in a sauce pan over medium heat until melted
  3. Remove from heat, add the rest of the ingrediets in the order above
  4. Pour into a 9in greased baking pan
  5. Bake for 18-20 minutes

Notes:

  • For more dense and fudgy brownies, cook for only 18 minutes and put into fridge immediately
  • Reccomended to be served cold, and great to serve with a scoop of ice cream

Ingredients:

  • 2tbs Butter
  • 1.5oz 100% cocoa solid bakers chocolate
  • 1.5c Confectioners Sugar
  • 1tsp Vanilla
  • Up to 3tbs Hot Water

Directions:

  1. Melt chocolate and butter over low heat and, remove when melted together
  2. Stir in Confectioners sugar and Vanilla (The batter will be very dry and lumpy)
  3. Begin adding water in a little at a time, stirring thuroughly after each addition
  4. Continue adding small bits of water and beating until a creamy and shiny texture is achieved

Notes:

  • Spread immediately after making, as it will start setting very quickly

Ingredients:

  • 1/2c Unsalted Butter
  • 3/4c Shortening
  • 2c Sugar
  • 4c Flour
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1/2c Molasses
  • 2tsp Baking Soda
  • 2tsp Cinnamon
  • 2tsp Ginger
  • 1/2tsp Cloves
  • Salt to taste
  • Granulated Sugar for coating the cookies

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°
  2. Cream butter and shortening together
  3. Add sugar and combine until mixture is fluffy
  4. Beat in eggs one at a time until incorporated
  5. Add in the Molasses
  6. Stir in the salt, baking soda, and spices
  7. Slowly stir in flour one cup at a time
  8. Chill dough in the fridge for at least an hour
  9. Form into 1" balls and roll in granulated sugar
  10. Place the balls about 2" apart on a greased cookie sheet
  11. Bake for 12-15 minutes

Notes:

  • Granulated Sugar or Sugar in the Raw can be used to coat the cookies
  • Cookies should be allowed to cool for 3-4 minutes before being removed from the cookie sheet
